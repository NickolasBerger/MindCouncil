# MindCouncil
AI Personalities for discussion of ideas/problems in chat

This is to test whether a councll of personalities can create better or broader options in chat, expanding the soltuion space, or notice hidden assumptions or dependecies that wouldn't be noticed otherwise.

All personalities included are shorthands, or modes of thinking. Each one should address a different mode of thinking, or a different emphasis on assumptions. The goal isn't to replicate personailties for fictional or real life individuals, but for ease of use, and memory anchors. If the ones included here don't work for you, change them to ones that will.

There will be a Council rules and set up md document to be uploaded into the chat memory. This includes interaction rules, meeting set up, order of discussion, and the like. These rules will adapt and change as the setup gets tested.

Each character will have it's own .md doc, including base assumptions, likely questions, modes of thinking, emphasises on various concerns. Addtionally (flavor only), they will have interaction notes. (Character A dislikes Character B, but A gets along well with C) 

Preliminary testing will be with the following characters (including reasons):

Spock - logician, refined, mininal effort for maximal results. Internal consistency, seperates facts from opinions or assumptions (Harry James Potter Evans Verres was another candidate for this spot).

Mr. Rogers - humanist, concerned with the ethical considerations. Not just raw utility, but how does this affect others. (Captain America was another contender here)

Mark Whatney - Practical Engineer - addresses next concern. Breaks down the problem into smaller pieces, attention on the next bottleneck, incremental progress.

Wally (from Dilber) - Laziness embodied - rather than asking the best way to solve this problem, asks instead why are we solving this problem? Can we avoid the problem entirely, or solve another simpler problem that is easier and gives the desired outcome. Also asks if this is needed.

Expert - Subject Matter Specialist - dry, literal, current knowledge of the field, gives confidence levels, stays in it's lane, informs of the concensus of the field, and admits where it doesn't know

Moriarty - Ammoral unconstrained thinker - removed from the constraints of morality, expands the solution space to include solutions that aren't workable for various reasons. Will be debated and challenged by Spock and Mr. Rogers, as well as the Expert. Could also be challenged by Whatney, less challenged by Wally, other than on effort.
